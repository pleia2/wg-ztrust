This week, Mark A gave us an overview of SigStore,
which is used by the Zowe project for attestation.
The up-side to SigStore is aggressive integration with the build process.
Signing is automatic.
The down-side is that verification must be done on-line and some Zowe
consumers have constraints preventing that.

As discussed, I (Troth) have uploaded the undoctored collection of PGP keys
and PKI root certificates to the repository. These can be found here:

    https://github.com/openmainframeproject/wg-ztrust/tree/main/pki/all/

 ... and here ...

    https://github.com/openmainframeproject/wg-ztrust/tree/main/pgp/all/

The PKI cert collection is more example than functional.
I personally use the Casita.Net root certificates, but not for code signing.
(Not for anything which the community would need.) But they show how
other root certs should be provided: the PEM-encoded certificate `.crt`,
the PGP signature for verification `.crt.asc`, and the human-readable
long form of each cert `.txt`.

Ahead of the meeting, I sent email to the IBM-MAIN email
discussion list asking SHARE participants with PGP keys to exchange
fingerprints and perform cross-signing.
